# Candidate Dropout Playbook

## Situation
A candidate who previously showed strong interest has become unresponsive, delayed their responses significantly, or explicitly mentioned competing offers. The candidate is at risk of dropping out of the pipeline before an offer is extended or accepted.

## Guidance
Candidate dropout is the recruitment equivalent of customer churn. The signals are often subtle — response times lengthening from hours to days, vague answers about timeline, or sudden requests to "pause" the process. Act within 24–48 hours of detecting disengagement. The goal is to either re-engage with urgency or qualify out gracefully so the pipeline stays clean.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Send a personal, non-templated follow-up** from the hiring manager (not just the recruiter) — this signals seniority and genuine interest.
2. **Surface the candidate's top motivators** from earlier conversations and address them directly — if they mentioned growth opportunities, share a concrete career path example.
3. **Compress the remaining process** — if there are 2 more rounds, explore whether they can be combined or fast-tracked to reduce friction.
4. **Ask directly about competing timelines** — "We want to make sure our process respects your timeline. Are you evaluating other opportunities with upcoming deadlines?"
5. **Set a clear decision deadline** — ambiguity kills urgency. Communicate when you need their answer and why.
