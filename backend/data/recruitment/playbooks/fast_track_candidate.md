# Fast-Track Candidate Playbook

## Situation
A candidate has demonstrated exceptional performance in interviews, strong cultural alignment, and rare skill-set match. There is a high risk of losing them to competing offers if the process is not accelerated. The hiring team has consensus that this is a top-tier candidate.

## Guidance
Fast-tracking is about removing unnecessary friction without lowering the bar. The candidate has already proven themselves — the remaining steps should be about mutual fit confirmation, not additional gatekeeping. Every day of delay increases the probability of a competing offer being accepted. Coordinate internally before communicating externally so the candidate experiences a seamless, confident process.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Consolidate remaining interviews** into a single day or half-day panel to minimize calendar drag.
2. **Pre-approve the compensation band** with the hiring manager and finance so an offer can be extended within 24 hours of final approval.
3. **Assign a senior team member as a "closer"** — someone who can have a candid conversation about the role, team culture, and growth trajectory.
4. **Communicate the accelerated timeline to the candidate explicitly** — "We're very impressed and want to move quickly. Here's our plan for this week."
5. **Prepare the offer letter in parallel** with final interviews so there is zero lag between approval and delivery.
