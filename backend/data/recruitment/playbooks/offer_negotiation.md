# Offer Negotiation Playbook

## Situation
An offer has been extended and the candidate has entered negotiation — requesting higher compensation, different title, remote flexibility, signing bonus, or accelerated review cycle. The candidate has not yet accepted or declined.

## Guidance
Negotiation is a positive signal — it means the candidate is seriously considering the role. The goal is to find a mutually acceptable outcome without setting precedents that create internal equity issues. Be flexible on non-cash elements (title, remote days, start date) which often cost the company little but matter significantly to the candidate. Always have a walk-away number defined before entering negotiation.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Acknowledge the counter professionally** — "Thank you for sharing what's important to you. Let me review this with our team and get back to you within 48 hours."
2. **Separate negotiable from non-negotiable items** — base salary bands may be fixed, but signing bonus, equity refresh, start date, and title often have flexibility.
3. **Prepare a "total compensation" view** that includes benefits, equity, learning budget, and perks — candidates often undervalue non-cash components.
4. **Set a response deadline** — open-ended negotiations lose momentum. "We'd love to finalize by Friday so we can get your onboarding started."
5. **Have the hiring manager make the final pitch** — a personal call from the future manager expressing genuine excitement is often the decisive factor, not the last $5K.
