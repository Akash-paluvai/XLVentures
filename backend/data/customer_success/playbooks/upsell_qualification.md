# Upsell Qualification Playbook

## Situation
The customer's usage is trending upward, they are approaching or exceeding their current plan limits, or they have expressed interest in expanding to additional teams or use cases.

## Guidance
Upsell conversations should feel like a natural extension of the customer's success, not a sales pitch. Wait until there is clear evidence of value realization before introducing expansion options. The strongest upsell signals are organic — the customer is hitting limits because they are getting real value.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Quantify the growth signal** — document current usage vs. plan limits, new team adoption, and feature utilization trends to build a data-driven case.
2. **Schedule a QBR (Quarterly Business Review)** that positions the expansion conversation within a strategic framework rather than a transactional upsell.
3. **Map the expansion to their business goals** — connect the higher plan tier or add-on to specific outcomes they care about (e.g., faster time-to-insight, more seats for the EMEA team).
4. **Prepare a comparison matrix** showing the value gap between their current plan and the recommended upgrade.
5. **Involve your account executive** early so pricing and contract mechanics are handled cleanly without the CSM becoming a negotiator.
