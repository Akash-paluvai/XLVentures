# Champion Change Playbook

## Situation
The primary champion or executive sponsor at the customer organization has left the company, changed roles, or become unreachable. This is often the most dangerous signal for account retention because the new stakeholder may not understand or value the platform.

## Guidance
Speed is critical. The window between a champion departure and a new stakeholder forming opinions about vendor relationships is typically 2–4 weeks. If you are not in the room early, a competitor or internal "build vs. buy" narrative will fill the vacuum. Treat this as an emergency even if the account health score was previously strong.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Identify the new stakeholder immediately** — check LinkedIn, ask your day-to-day contacts, or escalate through your internal executive network to get an introduction.
2. **Request a "re-onboarding" meeting** positioned as a courtesy — the goal is to understand the new stakeholder's priorities and demonstrate ongoing value without assuming they know the history.
3. **Prepare a relationship handoff document** summarizing the account's journey, key wins, open initiatives, and agreed-upon success metrics.
4. **Engage a senior leader from your own organization** to participate in the introductory call — this signals the importance of the relationship.
5. **Accelerate any pending value deliverables** — if there were planned features, integrations, or training sessions, move them up to create early momentum with the new champion.
