# Escalation Handling Playbook

## Situation
The customer has raised a critical support ticket, reported an SLA breach, or their executive team has sent escalation communications demanding immediate resolution. The relationship is at risk of irreversible damage.

## Guidance
Escalations are trust emergencies. The customer's frustration is not just about the technical issue — it is about whether they can rely on your organization. The response must demonstrate competence, urgency, and accountability simultaneously. Never deflect blame; instead, own the narrative and show a clear path to resolution.

## Recommended Actions
1. **Acknowledge the issue within 2 hours** with a named owner (not a ticket auto-reply) — the customer needs to know a real person is driving resolution.
2. **Provide a root cause timeline** even if preliminary — "We are investigating" is not enough; share what you know, what you don't, and when the next update will come.
3. **Escalate internally to engineering leadership** and create a dedicated war-room channel for real-time coordination.
4. **Schedule a follow-up call with the customer within 24 hours** to present the root cause analysis and remediation plan, including what safeguards will prevent recurrence.
5. **Prepare a service credit or remediation offer** proactively — do not wait for the customer to demand compensation.
